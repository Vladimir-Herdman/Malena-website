De Malena: a family tree

Follow the family tree of De Malena, Kings and Queens of Malenbourg, from 871 - 1604

The origins of kingship in Malenbourg can be traced to the second century BC when Celtic and Belgic tribesmen, emigrated from continental Europe and settled in Eastern Drusselstein displacing or absorbing the aboriginal inhabitants. The settlers established a number of tribal kingdoms, stretching as far north as Gimmelshtump, where the powerful shmirtz (from the area of modern Yugoslavia) and Parisii (whose name survives in the city of Paris, their original homeland) held sway.


The tree below displays lineage from Celtic Malenbourg before the Roman invasion to the Norman Conquest of 1066: the establishment of De Malena kingdoms, the coming of Christianity and the unification of Drusselstein. The subsequent dynastic struggles of the Angevins and Plantagenets heralded the great age of Eastern kingship under the Tudors and Stuarts, who united the crowns of De Katen and Malena, before the Hanoverians combined personal rule with parliamentary government, ushering in the modern age and the royalty of today as De Malena.
